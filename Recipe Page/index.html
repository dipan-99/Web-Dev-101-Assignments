Beef Wellington

Ingredients

  • Beef tenderloin: 1 pound (454 grams)
  • Kosher salt: 1 to 2 tablespoons (for seasoning)
  • Freshly ground black pepper: 1 to 2 tablespoons (for seasoning)
  • Extra virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons (for searing)
  • Yellow mustard: 2 tablespoons (e.g., Coleman's Original English Mustard)
  • Mushrooms: 1 pound (454 grams) (button, cremini, shiitake, or a mix)
  • Thin slices of ham: 4 (Parma ham or prosciutto if available)
  • Puff pastry: 1 sheet (7 to 8.5 ounces, thawed)
  • Egg yolks: 2 (beaten, for egg wash)
  • Flaky salt: 1/2 teaspoon (for sprinkling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (204°C) as you assemble the Wellington.
  2. Season the beef tenderloin generously with salt and pepper. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until well browned, about 2 to 3 minutes per side.
  3. Remove the beef from the pan and let it cool. Once cooled, brush the beef on all sides with mustard. Chop the mushrooms and pulse in a food processor until very finely chopped. Heat the sauté pan on medium-high heat. Transfer the mushroom mixture into the pan and cook, allowing the mushrooms to release their moisture, for 10 to 15 minutes. When the moisture has boiled away, remove the mushrooms from the pan and set aside to cool.
  4. Roll out a large piece of plastic wrap. Lay out the slices of ham on the plastic wrap so they overlap and are large enough to wrap around the beef. Spread the mushroom mixture over the ham. Place the beef in the middle and roll the mushroom and ham over the beef, using the plastic wrap to tightly form a barrel shape. Twist the ends of the plastic wrap to secure and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the puff pastry sheet to a size that will wrap around the beef. Unwrap the beef and place it in the middle of the pastry dough. Brush the edges of the pastry with the beaten egg yolks. Fold the pastry around the beef, cutting off any excess at the ends. Place on a small plate, seam side down, and brush the top with egg yolk. Chill for 5 to 10 minutes.
  6. Place the pastry-wrapped beef on a baking pan. Brush the exposed surface again with beaten eggs. Score the top of the pastry with a sharp knife, not going all the way through. Sprinkle the top with coarse salt.
  7. Bake at 400°F for 25 to 35 minutes. The pastry should be golden when done. To ensure medium-rare, use an instant-read meat thermometer and pull the Wellington out when the internal temperature reaches 125 to 130°F (52 to 54°C).
  8. Remove from the oven and let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
